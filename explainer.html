Bosch etch & Cu fill: what actually controls the outcome

HBM stacks depend on TSVs -- microscopic vertical tunnels etched through silicon and filled with copper to connect stacked memory dies. Get the etch or the fill wrong and the via comes out crooked, scalloped, or with a trapped void that cracks the chip later. This is a data-driven search for what actually controls those outcomes, using ViennaPS to simulate the real process physics.

Bottom line

A 768-run, 4-parameter DOE found that etch_time (per-cycle duration) — not the parameter most people would guess first — is the dominant control on TSV wall straightness. Applying the DOE-winning recipe and re-tuning every downstream step (liner, barrier, Cu fill) against it produced a genuine ~2.8x improvement in wall straightness over the starting point.

Two real mistakes were caught and fixed along the way, not glossed over: the raw sweep's best-looking result was partly an artifact of measuring a shallower via (corrected via depth-matched replicates, below); and an early "defect-free" Cu fill wasn't actually reaching the via floor (found by testing it, then fixed with a proper parameter sweep).

Scope: this models etch/deposition topography at single-feature scale. It does not model package warpage, TCB/hybrid-bonding thermal reliability, or JEDEC signaling — see the notebook README for the full picture.

768-run DOE: which parameter actually matters

4 parameters (ion_source_exponent, neutral_sticking_probability, etch_time, deposition_thickness) at 8x8x4x3 = 768 combinations. etch_time turned out to dominate -- bigger than the isotropic component a smaller 2-parameter sweep had suggested was the main knob.

Methodology note: the raw sweep's top result looked dramatically better than it really was -- short etch_time at a fixed cycle count means a shallower via, and shallower vias trivially show less wall bulge regardless of whether the recipe is actually better. Verified by regenerating the winner at a depth-matched cycle count across 3 replicates (needed because these are Monte Carlo ray-traced runs with real run-to-run noise): a genuine ~2.8x improvement survives the correction, just not the ~3.7x the confounded raw number implied.
