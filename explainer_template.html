Using ViennaPS to research a complete TSV traveler

See how recipe settings reshape a TSV through patterning, Bosch etch, liner, barrier and seed, Cu fill, and CMP. The case study follows 1,948 simulated travelers from the first screening run to the final recommendation.

The inputs are ViennaPS model coefficients, not wafer-qualified tool recipes. The study covers single-via geometry; it does not cover package warpage, bonding reliability, or signaling.

What ViennaPS-HBM can show

METHOD

Why we ran nine experiments instead of one sweep

We searched broadly first, then used each result to choose the next experiment. Random runs were repeated, recipe comparisons started from the same etched shape, and any best setting at the edge of a range was tested beyond that edge.

1. List the settingsFind every input the current workflow can change.
2. Search broadlyTry many combinations across wide ranges, with four repeats each.
3. Compare fairlyGive downstream recipes the same etched via so random shape differences do not bias the result.
4. Test the edgesExpand a range when its best result sits at the minimum or maximum.
5. Repeat the bestUse sixteen new etched shapes before recommending a recipe.

Select an experiment to see the question it answered. The internal loss chart was removed because scores from different search ranges were not directly comparable.

RESULT

Four steps pass. Cu fill and CMP do not.

saved full-process simulations support this result. The two best recipes pass the first four steps in all sixteen final repeats. Cu fill and CMP fail every time.

StepWhat counts as a passFinal result

Why Cu fill fails

The model leaves a centerline void in every repeat. The final two recipes leave gaps of 0.134 to 0.239 model length; the target is zero.

Why CMP fails

Uniform removal leaves the Cu surface recessed by 0.303 to 0.355 model length; the target is zero. Removing more material destroys the mask.

The next step is to add fill chemistry and CMP pad-pressure effects to the simulator, then calibrate them with measurements.

LEARNINGS

Five rules worth carrying into the next study

Carry the shape forwardA small DRIE change can alter liner coverage, Cu fill, and CMP.
Compare the same viaUse the same etched shape when testing downstream recipes.
Respect failure rulesA lower dish is not useful if CMP destroys the mask.
Test beyond an edgeIf the best setting is the largest or smallest tested, expand the range.
Know when to change modelsMore runs cannot recover physics that the simulator does not contain.

Practical use: narrow physical experiments, identify sensitive recipe families, and catch recipes that win through a scoring artifact. Calibration data is still required before transferring settings to a fab.

HISTORY

What the earlier studies changed

Earlier etch and fill studies exposed three mistakes: comparing etches at different depths, trusting one random run, and calling an edge setting optimal before expanding the range. The nine experiments above apply those corrections throughout.
